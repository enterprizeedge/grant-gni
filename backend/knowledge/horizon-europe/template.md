# Horizon Europe — Standard Application Form (Part B) structure

Horizon Europe Research and Innovation Actions (RIA) and Innovation Actions (IA)
are evaluated against three award criteria, each scored 0–5 (threshold 3, overall
threshold 10). Part B has a 45-page limit for sections 1–3.

## Section 1 — Excellence
[section: excellence]
Covers: objectives and ambition; relation to the work programme; concept and
methodology; how the project goes beyond the state of the art.

Evaluation focus:
- Clarity and pertinence of the project's objectives, and the extent to which they
  are ambitious and go beyond the state of the art.
- Soundness of the methodology, including consideration of inter/multidisciplinary
  approaches, gender dimension in research content, and open science practices.

Required elements:
1.1 Objectives and ambition (measurable, verifiable, linked to call scope)
1.2 Methodology (research approach, work concept, risk-aware design)
1.3 Beyond state of the art (clear baseline + quantified advance)

## Section 2 — Impact
[section: impact]
Covers: the project's pathways towards impact; the scale and significance of
expected outcomes; measures to maximise impact (dissemination, exploitation,
communication).

Evaluation focus:
- Credibility of the pathways to achieve the expected outcomes and impacts in the
  work programme, and the likely scale and significance of the contributions.
- Suitability and quality of the measures to maximise expected outcomes and impacts.

Required elements:
2.1 Project's pathways towards impact (outcomes -> impacts, with assumptions)
2.2 Measures to maximise impact — dissemination, exploitation, IPR, communication
2.3 Summary canvas (specific needs, expected results, target groups, outcomes, impacts)

## Section 3 — Quality and efficiency of the implementation
[section: implementation]
Covers: work plan and work packages; allocation of resources; consortium capacity.

Evaluation focus:
- Quality and effectiveness of the work plan, assessment of risks, and
  appropriateness of the effort assigned to work packages.
- Capacity and role of each participant, and the extent the consortium as a whole
  brings together the necessary expertise.

Required elements:
3.1 Work plan and resources (WP list, deliverables, milestones, Gantt, PM table)
3.2 Capacity of participants and consortium as a whole

## Mandatory annexes / checks
- Ethics self-assessment, security, gender equality plan (eligibility for many
  beneficiaries), open science, data management (DMP if data generated).
