# Exemplar 1 — Work plan and resources (scored 5/5)

The work plan comprises six work packages with clear interdependencies shown in a
PERT chart. Each WP has SMART objectives, a lead beneficiary, listed deliverables and
milestones with verifiable acceptance criteria, and effort justified in person-months.
A dedicated risk table lists each critical risk, its likelihood/severity, the WP that
owns it, and a concrete mitigation with a fallback. Effort allocation is justified
against the ambition: 40% to the pilot demonstration WP, reflecting that the IA's
value lies in validation in a relevant environment.

# Exemplar 2 — Consortium capacity (scored 5/5)

The consortium is presented as complementary rather than a list of CVs. A capability
matrix maps each required competence (electrolyser stacks, power electronics, steel
process integration, techno-economic modelling, exploitation) to the partner that
provides it, demonstrating no gaps and no redundancy. The inclusion of an industrial
off-taker from a hard-to-abate sector is highlighted as directly responding to the
call's eligibility expectation. Roles are non-overlapping and each partner's
contribution is essential to the project objectives.

# Common reviewer weaknesses this section avoids
- Effort not justified against WP ambition (reviewers flag generic PM distribution).
- Risk tables that list risks without owners or concrete mitigations.
- Consortium described as CVs rather than complementary capability coverage.
- Deliverables without verifiable acceptance criteria.
