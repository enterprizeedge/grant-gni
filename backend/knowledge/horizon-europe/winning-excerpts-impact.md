# Exemplar 1 — Pathways towards impact (scored 5/5)

We articulate impact as an explicit chain: project results -> outcomes -> impacts,
stating the assumptions at each step. For example: the validated low-iridium stack
(result) enables European manufacturers to produce electrolysers with 70% less
critical raw material (outcome), which contributes to EU supply-chain resilience and
the 2030 renewable-hydrogen target (impact). Each link names the target group (steel
off-takers, electrolyser OEMs, EU policymakers) and the barrier the project removes.

Crucially, we connect technical KPIs to the funder's strategic intent: by quantifying
the contribution to the REPowerEU hydrogen target (0.4 Mt/year potential at scale) we
demonstrate policy relevance, not just technical merit. Reviewers of this topic
reward proposals that make the EU-level significance explicit.

# Exemplar 2 — Measures to maximise impact (scored 5/5)

The dissemination, exploitation and communication (DEC) plan is presented as a table
with concrete channels, audiences, KPIs, timing and responsible partners. Exploitation
includes a freedom-to-operate analysis, a joint IPR agreement summarised in the
consortium agreement, and three named key exploitable results each with an owner and
a route to market. A standardisation strategy commits to contributing to two ISO/IEC
working groups. Communication targets quantified reach with measurable indicators
rather than vague "we will raise awareness" statements.
