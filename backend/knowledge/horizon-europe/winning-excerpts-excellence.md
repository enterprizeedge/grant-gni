# Exemplar 1 — Objectives and ambition (scored 5/5)

The overall objective of HYDRA-FLEX is to demonstrate a dynamically operated 50 MW
PEM electrolysis system that produces renewable hydrogen at EUR 2.8/kg by project
end, validated in a steel-plant environment. This objective is decomposed into four
SMART objectives, each with a quantified target, a verification method, and a
responsible work package:

- O1: Raise stack durability from 40,000 h to 60,000 h under dynamic load (verified
  by accelerated stress testing, WP2).
- O2: Cut iridium loading by 70% versus the 2024 baseline of 1.5 mg/cm² (verified by
  XRF and performance retention, WP3).
- O3: Demonstrate <2 s ramp response to a 0–100% load step (verified on the pilot,
  WP4).
- O4: Reduce levelised cost of hydrogen to EUR 2.8/kg (verified by an audited
  techno-economic model, WP5).

Ambition is expressed against a clearly quantified state of the art: today's best
demonstrated systems achieve EUR 4.5/kg at 40,000 h durability with 1.5 mg/cm²
iridium loading. HYDRA-FLEX advances all three dimensions simultaneously, which no
prior project has achieved.

# Exemplar 2 — Beyond the state of the art (scored 5/5)

We summarise the advance in a single table mapping each innovation to the current
baseline, the project target, and the quantified delta. Rather than claiming general
"novelty", we tie each claim to a measurable KPI and cite the specific limitation in
the current literature it overcomes. The methodology explicitly integrates the gender
dimension by analysing how operator interface design affects safety outcomes across
a gender-balanced operator cohort, and adopts open-science practices by committing to
publish all non-confidential datasets under FAIR principles.
